Should we step in or out of comfort zone?

Le 18/10/2019


Most of us adults feel stress or anxiety for some time at least daily. It is the reaction towards the unknown based on the fears of losing or the probability of a negative outcome. Nothing beats this feelings like real live action.


Breaking out to face our fears for the new world experiences to explore and be adventurous is human nature. Tasting different foods, facing the camera, traveling to places we haven’t been before and making connections pushes us to new boundaries. Having written that, it is also our innate character to desire to be in a neutral state with fewer levels of anxiety and stress.



/“Do one thing everyday that scares you. — Eleanor Roosevelt.”/


Words motivating us to get out of our comfort zone are flooding on Instagram and elsewhere. It adorns the spaces of wall arts to tattoos to coffee mugs. There are also a few rebels slowly starting to express against coming out of their zones and they are happy to be in it. Now, what is the decision we should make? Should we stay in or step out of the comfort zone?


Defining the comfort zone

Firstly, the term comfort zone origination is hard to be traced. Many sources say it could be from the temperature range within which one feels cozy. The comfort zone is the plane at which we act with a lack of difficulty and familiarity. Probably gained through routine and therefore, it is no more unusual or surprising.


In 1907, psychobiologist Robert Yerkes who studied animal and human behavior had tested with mice that portrayed ‘their innate habit of running around, describing greater or smaller circles or more frequently whirling around on the same spot with incredible rapidity’.


He concluded that in mice, ‘anxiety improves performance until a certain optimum level of arousal has been reached’. From which we can understand that to perform highly, one has to undergo good stress. To raise your focus and drive which will eventually sustain you in sudden unanticipated situations. He also stated, ‘beyond the optimal point, performance deteriorates as higher levels of anxiety are attained’.


The dancing mouse study reveals that it is important to step out of your comfort zone and it benefits one. Also, what I understand from this behavior of mice is after reaching out we need to rest to avoid burnout. So it is getting back to our comfort zone. I would say it is best to go the Buddha way here. Stick to balance. We need to get to our psychological state where we can be most true to ourselves, rest and recharge.


In 2009, Management theorist Alasdair White, examined the [comfort zone theory](http://www.whiteandmaclean.eu/uploaded_files/120120109110852performance_management-final290110(2)-preview.pdf) where he concluded, “Both motivation and anxiety are, behaviorally, sub-sets of stress and, consequently, they are tools to assist in performance management — there will be times when motivation will be the most useful tool, while at others the introduction of anxiety will be more appropriate. However, too much motivation or anxiety will result in too much stress and this will result in performance being disabled”.


Though, a 2017 study reveals there is no facts to back this conclusion. But, again this management theory reveals good stress within the correct levels can be best practice models in workplaces.


We have our convenient territories, each different from one. The to and fro activity blurs our different zone boundaries leading to the main objective. It is to achieve the optimal level so that we get used to our new sphere to expand our comfort zone.


Benefits of expanding your comfort zone.

It challenges one to walk on the path of personal development. It enhances mental health and increases your creativity and pace. We don’t necessarily have to do wild daring things like climbing Mount Everest, but simply doing some of our daily activities differently is a great place to start with. Taking a different route to work, reading an offbeat genre of book, having a new haircut or tapping into creativity by joining a glass painting class are some of the steps one could make.


Finally, we should remember to do what we want to do, with little changes every day and the needed amount of stress with rests. The circumference of our comfort level will increase making one feel alive and good.
